Jane’s Walk Weekend

May 1–3, FREE

Kick off National Preservation Month by getting to know some of Providence’s hidden treasures. This global festival is inspired by urban activist Jane Jacobs and takes place in over 200 cities during the first weekend in May. Unlike most guided tours, these free walking conversations are led by volunteer citizen guides who share knowledge of their own communities while inspiring participants to think, talk, and connect.

Marker Meet-up

May 14, 5:30 pm, FREE

PPS has publicly recognized over 1,500 buildings and property owners through its Historic Marker Program, and if we’ve learned anything, it’s that Providence’s historic buildings and the people who love them are endlessly interesting. Have a maker on your house already? Come mix and mingle and share the remarkable history of your home.

Want to know more about this longstanding program? We’ll be available to answer your questions and share information about the city’s awesome architecture.

Bite-Sized Preservation

May 21, 12:00 pm, FREE
24 Meeting Street (PPS Offices)

Join us for our Bite-Sized Preservation discussion series. Pack a lunchpail and stop by the Old Brick School House for just as much preservation as fits in a lunch hour. Each month will bring a new topic and slate of advocacy updates.

Neighborhood History Lecture

May 26, 5:30 pm, FREE (registration required)
20 Washington Place (RISD Administration Offices)

James Barnes, AIA, will whet our appetites for the history and architecture of Keene Street and Lloyd Avenue.

Music in the Garden

May 27, 6:00 pm
$20 general, $15 PPS members, $10 Mile of History Association members
21 Meeting Street

Come enjoy the first of our summer garden concerts, which launches in conjunction with the Festival. This series takes place on the last Wednesday of the month from May through September and will feature an eclectic range of performers from classical to contemporary. First up is Cowboy and Lady, a country-blues-inspired duo.

About PPS

The Providence Preservation Society is a non-profit organization based in Providence, Rhode Island whose mission is to improve Providence by advocating for historic preservation and the enhancement of the city’s unique character through thoughtful planning and design.
