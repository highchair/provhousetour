Travel & Lodging

Providence has a wealth of boutique hotels in historic buildings that may appeal to Festival-goers who are coming from afar! Please see our curated list below.

Beatrice Hotel

Book at the Beatrice Hotel and save 15%. Ranked the #1 hotel in Providence on TripAdvisor and named among the Top 13 Hotels in New England by Condé Nast Traveler.

Modernism Festival attendees who make a reservation at the Beatrice Hotel using the above link will receive a 15% discount off the hotel’s best available rate with a token to redeem a welcome signature Bellini cocktail in the Bellini restaurant or Bellini rooftop!

The Beatrice Hotel, formerly the Exchange Building (90 Westminster Street). Built 1845, redesigned in 1887; architects: Stone, Carpenter & Wilson. Renovated in 2021 by Eric Zuena and ZDS Architecture & Interiors. Recipient of the 2021 American Institute of Architects Rhode Island Interior Architecture Design Award, 2022 Rhode Island Design Award for Commercial New Construction/Renovation (Gold), and 2022 Rhode Island Design Award for Commercial Interior Design (Gold); the restaurant, Bellini Providence by Ignazio & Maggio Cipriani received a 2022 Rhode Island Design Awards for Commercial Interior Design (Gold).

The Graduate by Hilton

Book at the Graduate. Formerly the Biltmore Hotel (11 Dorrance Street). Built 1922; architects: Warren & Wetmore. Closed in 1975 and reopened in 1979 with its signature elevator, on the exterior between two wings.

The Neptune Hotel

Book at The Neptune. Formerly the Diocese House, The Rialto, Pilgrim Inn, Civic View Inn, Church House Inn, Sportsman’s Inn, and the Dean Hotel (122 Fountain Street). Built 1915, architect unknown. Renovated by ASH NYC and KITE Architects in 2014.

Dye House

Book at the Dye House. Formerly part of the Weybosset Mill Complex, American Woolen, Welsh Manufacturing Company, and American Trucking Company (46 Dyke Street). Built in 1888, renovated by Studio AD and KITE Architects.
