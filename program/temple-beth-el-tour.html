Wednesday, October 8

Temple Beth-El Tour with George Goodwin

Join PPS for a tour of Temple Beth-El with George Goodwin, author of the 1993 article, “The Design of a Modern Synagogue: Percival Goodman's Beth-El in Providence, Rhode Island.” Goodwin will relate the story of how Goodman came to be chosen over Erich Mendelsohn and how the design evolved from 1947 to 1951, when the cornerstone was laid. Goodwin will also discuss the artwork that was commissioned for the Temple, including by artists Herbert Ferber, David Hare, Dorothy Liebes, Ibram Lassaw, and Walter Feldman.

Speaker Bio

George M. Goodwin has edited the annual journal Rhode Island Jewish Historical Notes since 2004. He has written for numerous journals, including American Jewish Archives, American Jewish History, Modern Judaism, and Rhode Island History. He wrote the articles on “Rhode Island” and “Newport” for the Encyclopedia Judaica and coedited The Jews of Rhode Island, which was published by Brandeis University Press and the University Press of New England. Goodwin studied art history at Lake Forest College, earned a master’s degree at Columbia University and, 50 years ago, a Ph.D. at Stanford. He later earned a master’s in Jewish communal studies at Hebrew Union College.
