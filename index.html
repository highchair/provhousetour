Ticket sales for the Festival of Historic Houses have now ended. The self-guided house and garden tours will take place in the Mount Hope neighborhood on Saturday, June 8, from 10 am – 4 pm. The Festival is held rain or shine!

Information for attendees:

>All guests must first check in at the Registration Center, located at The Groden Center (86 Mt. Hope Avenue). Your name will be on the guest list at the check-in table, where you will pick up your Festival guidemap. The guidemap serves as the entrance pass to all open properties. The Registration Center opens at 9:50 am. Portajohns and water are available on site. Free parking is available in the lot at the rear of the Registration Center (accessible via Cypress Street), as well as on surrounding streets. Please follow all posted signage.

This year’s ten open homes and gardens are located on Forest Street, Larch Street, Ivy Street, Cypress Street, and Catalpa Road, and are all within walking distance of each other. Some properties may have steep stairs or uneven surfaces, including sidewalks.

Indoor photography and/or videography, smoking, eating, drinking, and cell phone conversations are prohibited inside the properties. To protect flooring, high-heeled shoes may not be worn inside the properties; Homeowners may request shoe removal or use of shoe covers.

Please contact us at info@ppsri.org with any questions or concerns. We look forward to seeing you on Saturday!

With twenty-five official neighborhoods and countless historic homes, cultural landscapes, and private gardens, Providence is bursting with places to explore.

For more than 40 years, the Providence Preservation Society (PPS) has produced the Festival of Historic Houses as a way to get to know the streets and homes that you won’t necessarily find in any guidebook. Each year, the Festival highlights the architecture and history of one of Providence’s historic neighborhoods, drawing hundreds of people from near and far.

On Saturday, June 8, PPS will present the 42nd Festival of Historic Houses. This year, the self-guided tour will explore homes and gardens in the historic Mount Hope neighborhood, including Larch Street, Ivy Street, Forest Street, and Catalpa Road.

In addition to Saturday’s tours of this historic neighborhood, an exclusive Kickoff Party and several Festival walks and talks will be offered. We hope you will join us!
