This year, Providence Preservation Society’s Festival of Historic Houses goes MODERN — and stretches to an entire week of programs that explore modernism in Providence in the period 1950 to 1990.

Join us on Sunday, October 5 for the opening of the Festival of Modern Design with a morning walking tour of the modern, postmodern and contemporary architecture of Pratt Street.

That evening, the Festival’s keynote address will be given by the design historian and curator Donald Albrecht. Albrecht’s presentation, “Designing Home: Jews and Midcentury Modernism” will address the role Jewish designers, architects, and patrons played in shaping modernism in America.

Throughout the week, enjoy lectures, walks, panel conversations, and behind-the-scenes tours of modern furniture and decorative arts collections. Special events include the Mid Mod Cocktail Party on Thursday, October 9 at a 1959 Castellucci Galli home on the East Side; a symposium on Friday, October 10 titled Providence Modern (1945-1995): Architecture, Design, Planning, Culture; and our signature open house event on Saturday, October 11, which includes access to a dozen homes on the East Side designed by architects including Ira Rakatansky and Friedrich St Florian.

The Festival of Modern Design participates in Docomomo’s Tour Day on October 11, joining this nation-wide celebration of modernism with programs organized by architecture and preservation groups across the country.

News flash: We are seeking volunteer docents for the Open House on October 11. In exchange for a 3-hour volunteer shift at one of the open houses, volunteers receive a free ticket to the open house (a $75 value!). Fill out our Google form application!

Providence Preservation Society (PPS) is one of the city’s legacy cultural and civic organizations. Founded in 1956 on College Hill, we work across the city’s 25 neighborhoods to preserve Providence’s architectural landmarks, promote sustainable growth, and celebrate our unique history and heritage through research, education and advocacy. To find out more, visit ppsri.org.

The Festival of Modern Design benefits PPS’s year-round efforts to document, interpret, preserve and advocate for Providence’s architectural landmarks and beloved places. Thank you for being part of the work we do. We are grateful to the Festival’s event sponsors, including the Presenting Sponsor, Providence Tourism Council.
